How Climate Change Affects Our Land

Understanding the profound impacts of a warming planet on the ground beneath our feet.

Increased Drought and Wildfires

Rising global temperatures lead to more frequent and severe heatwaves, causing soil to lose moisture. This creates tinder-dry conditions, making landscapes more susceptible to wildfires. These fires not only destroy ecosystems and communities but also release vast amounts of carbon into the atmosphere, further accelerating climate change.

Land Degradation and Desertification

Climate change exacerbates the process of desertification, where fertile land becomes desert. Changes in rainfall patterns, combined with extreme heat, can strip the land of its vegetation and nutrients. This degradation reduces the land's ability to support agriculture, leading to food insecurity and displacing populations.

Shifting Agricultural Patterns

Farmers around the world are facing new challenges as traditional growing seasons shift. Some regions are becoming too hot or dry for their staple crops, while others may experience longer growing seasons. This unpredictability threatens global food supplies and requires significant adaptation in farming practices and crop choices.
